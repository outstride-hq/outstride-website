## 1. VOICE

Ranked by how far the page drifts from your voice.

1. **The six legacy-template tool pages read like a different product.**
   `symptom-map`, `company-health-scorecard`, `two-hour-constraint`, `ownership-map`,
   `human-9`, `leadership-hats` still use the old "Purpose / When to use it / How it works /
   Coaching questions / Output / artefact / Tags" skeleton. Two problems: (a) the clinical
   consultancy register ("Output / artefact", "Reveal highest-leverage founder work and
   expose low-value activity") is the opposite of how the newer pages talk; (b) each ends
   with a **"Tags" table that duplicates the metadata chips** the page already renders from
   `os.ts`. Every page built since the whiteboard batch (stakeholder-map, company-7,
   commitment-loop, the 1:1 suite) shows what the house style now is. Suggested fix: strip
   Tags + Output sections everywhere, rewrite the six intros/openers in first person.

2. **`master-my-mindset` is written from thin air.** No intake section exists at all; the
   hook, the "closer" founder anecdote and the framing are all invented. It's a decent page —
   but it's the only rich page with zero provenance trail, which breaks your own
   non-negotiable rule (Q1/Q2/Q5 before drafting). Either voice-memo it or accept it
   knowingly. Same problem in milder form: `map-the-destination`, `chart-the-path`,
   `install-the-operating-rhythm`, `make-the-right-calls` have no intake sections
   (`install-the-operating-rhythm`'s section exists but all seven answers are blank).
   `build-my-growth-system`'s intake has Q1 quotes but **no provenance note** — confirm
   whether those three quotes are real founder lines or drafted.

3. **Distinctive phrasings — mostly survived, three losses.** Verified verbatim on-page:
   "delegate or die", "go outside and play", "Champagne at 40", "sprinkle… founder magic",
   "marathon with periods of sprinting", "Nothing gets done unless I personally make sure it
   gets done", "Bullshit — there's always…", "delegate, don't abdicate", "give away your
   Legos". Lost or diluted:
   - **"I love OKRs plus traffic lighting"** — intake flags it as your line; the
     team-traffic-lighting page flattens it to "pair it with OKRs". Cheap win to restore.
   - **"What an honour it is to manage people"** — the hats page keeps the
     lives-changed line but drops the honour framing that opens your memo; the page why is
     the closest thing to your voice in the group, worth one more beat.
   - **"strong opinions loosely held"** appears in make-the-right-calls as "Strong opinions,
     weakly held" — fine, but your fundraising usage (primary/backup strategy with a
     pre-decided pivot trigger) never made it onto find-new-capital, and it's the more
     distinctive application.

4. **Format fighting the material.** `make-the-right-calls` is the clearest case: ~6 block
   sections (stakes-matching, mindsets, alignment grid, frameworks list, 3 Cs, personal
   board callout) hanging off the fixed spine — the AGENTS spec says 0–2 earned blocks. It
   reads like a chapter wearing a template. Candidates: trim to two sections (stakes +
   mindsets) and push 3 Cs / Six Hats / alignment-autonomy detail down to their tool pages —
   or make it the second blog-style page. `face-my-shadow` (3 sections + image) and
   `map-the-destination` (2 big sections + 2 callouts) are borderline but each section earns
   its place better.

5. **The blog-style precedent doesn't actually exist in code.** AGENTS.md and the intake both
   describe `build-a-life-worth-scaling-for` as the blog-style precedent, but the page uses
   the standard fixed slots like everything else (its blocks are just more narrative).
   Either the blog variant was never built or it was reverted. Decide: build the variant, or
   update AGENTS.md so future sessions stop looking for a renderer that isn't there.

6. Minor gloss spots: `values-deck` ("It teaches range: enough to create edge, not so much
   that it creates chaos"), `kss-feedback` ("It is a clearer way to hold standards while
   preserving trust") — coach-speak lines no founder would say; both pages otherwise fine.

## 3. DUPLICATION & OVERLAP

Recommended owner first, then what the other pages should do.

1. **Traffic lighting** (worst offender — appears on 5 capability pages + 2 tools + 1 draft).
   - **Owner: `design-the-org` + the `team-traffic-lighting` tool** for rating *teams* and
     deciding where the founder dives.
   - `take-command-of-my-role` keeps the *movement* half (dive/force/form/flow, Grove's
     limiting step) — that's genuinely its material post-merge — but its
     "The founder who moves" paragraph and design-the-org's "roving repair crew" callout say
     the same thing in nearly the same words ("dive in and force it… leave when it flows").
     Keep the full version on take-command; cut design-the-org's callout to one sentence +
     the existing capabilityRef.
   - `make-ownership-stick` keeps only the **red-comes-with-a-plan norm** (results RAG, not
     team RAG) — currently clean, leave as is.
   - `build-leaders-not-dependencies` should drop its whatGoodLooksLike bullet 2 ("Teams
     traffic-light their quarterly goals…") — that's ownership-page material restated — and
     keep only co-created goals/accountability.
   - `install-the-operating-rhythm` keeps the *cadence* (fortnightly OKR traffic-light
     meeting) — fine, it's the venue, not the concept.
   - **Kill or merge the `rag-company-status` draft tool** — its description ("RAG view of
     company health and quarterly goals… where the founder needs to rove and repair") is
     team-traffic-lighting + OKR colours already covered elsewhere.
2. **Bottleneck question** (`design-the-org` / `take-command-of-my-role`). Actually well
   split today: design-the-org asks "what's the company's bottleneck — build, sell, deliver?"
   (structure); take-command asks Grove's "what's the *next* limiting step?" (attention).
   Keep both, but neither page tells the reader the other half exists — add one bridging
   sentence each way so it reads as one idea at two altitudes rather than a repeat.
3. **Co-created accountability** (`make-ownership-stick` / `build-leaders`). The ownership
   page's closing callout already declares the org/individual split — good. Build-leaders
   owns the "Would it be useful if I held you accountable?" move, but says it **twice on its
   own page** (whatGoodLooksLike bullet 3 and doNow action 4, near-verbatim). Cut it from
   whatGoodLooksLike; the doNow is the better home.
4. **"Build your own yacht" callout** — duplicated near-verbatim on
   `build-a-life-worth-scaling-for` (blocks) and `live-it-today` (tool). Owner: the tool
   (it's the exercise's punchline); on the capability, compress to one line inside the
   existing "Build your own yacht" paragraph.
5. **"Marathon with periods of sprinting"** — verbatim callout on both
   `build-resilience-and-recovery` and `two-equilibria-map`. Owner: the capability (it's a
   worldview line, not a tool instruction). Tool can end on the tripwire step instead.
6. **Company diagnostics trio** — `company-7` vs `company-health-scorecard` vs
   `rag-company-status`. company-7 already positions itself as the 10-minute version and
   links the scorecard as "the deeper version" ✓. The scorecard page (legacy template, §1.1)
   never links back to company-7. Third wheel: rag-company-status (see 3.1).
7. **Energy vs Resilience merge** — the open question from 2026-07-02 is still open, and the
   overlap has grown since: `two-equilibria-map`, `personal-manual`, `personal-playbook`,
   burnout scores and habit-stack tripwires all straddle both pages. If they stay separate,
   the clean line is: energy = designing the normal week; resilience = detecting and
   recovering from the bad state. Both pages mostly respect that already; a decision would
   stop the next drafting session from re-blurring it.
